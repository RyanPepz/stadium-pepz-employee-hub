First Two Weeks — The Floor

Running food, touching tables, and handling phones

▾

By the end of Week 2, you should be comfortable with all of the following:

  1. Running food — match dishes to table numbers. One ticket may cover multiple dishes, so always verify.
  2. Touching tables — check in at least twice per table. Ask how the food is, offer sauces.
  3. Pre-bussing — ask about boxes and clear empty dishes before guests leave. Your shift lead may call this "pruning."
  4. Answering phones — always answer: "Thank you for calling Stadium Pepz on State College! My name is ___, will this be for takeout or delivery?"
  5. Handling takeout orders — verify last name or phone number every time. Print the receipt and check off every item.
  6. Getting Google reviews — it comes naturally through great service. Script: "Thank you so much for coming in — any feedback you leave, good or bad, really helps us grow."
Our mission: Every guest leaves happy. If you think something would improve their experience, ask your shift lead.
2

Working Through the Stations

Cashier, then Wings

▾
  1. Cashier — take orders on Toast POS. First face guests see — be warm, accurate, and upsell naturally (desserts, drinks, Sauced & Tossed Potatoes). Keep your cash drawer clean.
  2. Wings Station — receive tickets and fill orders. You put wings in the oven; cooks handle desserts and appetizers. Keep your station clean — tossing wings gets messy.
See Cashier SOP and Wings Station SOP in Role Guides.
3

Final Stations

Oven, then Bar — you're almost there

▾
  1. Pizza Oven — you are quality control. Cut and dress every pizza to match the ticket exactly.
    • Small (8"): 4 slices
    • Medium (12"): 8 slices
    • Large (16"): 10 slices
    • Extra Large (18"): 12 slices
  2. Bar — a flex position. Pour beers correctly (16oz = ½" head, 34oz = 1" head), wash glassware, help with food running and takeout when slow. RBS certificate required before working this station.
See Oven SOP and Bar SOP in Role Guides.

Cashier

First point of contact for every guest
FOH
▾
Taking Orders
  1. Greet the guest before touching the POS
  2. Tap New Order and select order type (Dine-In / Takeout / Delivery)
  3. Collect required info: table number for dine-in; name + phone for takeout; name, phone, and full address for delivery
  4. Enter all items and modifiers carefully
  5. Review the full order before moving to payment
  6. Tap Pay, confirm total, accept payment (card/cash/gift card), close check
  7. For alcohol orders: always print the receipt and hand it to the guest to present at the bar
Upselling
  • "Would you like any drinks with that?"
  • "Can I add a Pepz Cookie or Cinnasquares for dessert?"
  • "Would you like to upgrade your potatoes to Sauced & Tossed?"
  • "Any ranch with that order?"
When the Register Is Slow

Help run food or assist with takeout orders — but always keep one eye on the door for incoming guests.

Cash Drawer

Keep it organized. Double-check change before handing it over. If you need change in the till, notify your shift lead immediately — don't wait until guests are waiting.

Accuracy over speed, every time.
🏃

Food Runner / Floor Management

Guest experience from first bite to last
FOH
▾
Running Food

Each dish comes out with a ticket showing the table number. One ticket may cover multiple dishes — always confirm the right items go to the right table before you walk out.

Touching Tables

Check in at least twice per table during their meal. Ask how the food is, offer extra sauces, see if they need anything. If a guest seems unhappy, notify your shift lead immediately.

Pre-Bussing ("Pruning")

Toward the end of the meal, ask about boxes and clear empty dishes. Fully bus the table as soon as guests leave — don't let it sit.

Trash Cans

Check throughout your shift. Replace the bag right away if one is full.

Getting Google Reviews

This is part of your role. Get your review card lanyard from your shift lead at the start of your shift. Return it before clocking out — it doesn't leave the building.

"Thank you so much for coming in today. We're trying to get more feedback from our community — the good, the bad, the ugly. Any feedback you leave really helps us grow."
You are the face of the guest experience. Every table interaction is a chance to earn a 5-star review.

Wings Station

Toss to order, bag everything, keep it clean
FOH
▾
Wings

Receive tickets from the printer, put wings in the oven, and toss them to order.

Appetizers & Seasonal Items

You also prepare: pizza rolls, breadsticks, cinnasquares, Pepz cookies, and seasonal items (love bites, pizza skulls, gingerbread pizza pockets). Cooks handle putting most desserts and appetizers in the oven for you.

Sauces & Sides

Most dishes come with sides of different sauces — always include them. Missing a sauce is one of the most common mistakes.

Bagging Orders
  1. Bag all completed items securely
  2. Attach the ticket to the outside of the bag
  3. Place finished orders on the counter in the ready orders area
Station Cleanliness

Tossing wings gets messy. Wipe down your prep surface consistently throughout the shift.

Fill orders completely, include every sauce, and keep the station clean. When your ticket volume is low, see how you can help another station.

Bar

Flex position — pour right, stay active on the floor
FOH
▾
RBS Required

You must have a valid RBS certificate on file before working this station. Email it to ryan@stadiumpepz.com.

Pouring Beer
  • Keep the glass tilted against the nozzle to minimize foam
  • 16oz beers: ~½ inch of head
  • 34oz beers: ~1 inch of head
Beer Buckets

5 beers + a scoop of ice. When handing over: "Do you need a bottle opener?" Provide a bar key if needed.

Alcohol Receipt Process

When guests are waiting on beers, ask for their receipt. Mark or slightly tear it to show it's been fulfilled, then pour.

Glassware

Wash 16oz, 34oz, and 60oz glasses/pitchers throughout the shift. Keep sinks at the right temperature. Always have cold glasses ready.

When the Bar Is Slow

The bar is a flex position — help run food or assist with takeout. Check in with your shift lead for anything else.

Pour right, keep glasses clean, and be a resource to the floor when traffic is light.
📦

Takeout / 3rd Party

Every order verified and complete before handoff
FOH
▾
Step-by-Step Process
  1. Greet the guest warmly — ask for the name on the order
  2. Locate the order — search in Orders Hub or payment terminal. Always verify by last name or phone number — duplicate names are common
  3. Check payment — look for a red "NOT PAID" banner. If unpaid: use handheld for card, POS for cash. Collect payment before proceeding.
  4. Print the receipt — use it to check off every item
  5. Verify the order: all entrées and sides, sauces and dressings (most commonly missed), drinks and add-ons, special instructions
  6. Large orders: include plates, napkins, and utensils automatically
  7. Hand off: confirm customer name one more time, then: "Everything should be in the bag, including sauces and sides."
3rd Party Drivers (DoorDash, Uber Eats, etc.)

The driver must confirm pickup in their app before you hand over the order. Never hand off to a driver who hasn't confirmed in their system.

Zero missing items, zero unprocessed payments, every time. When in doubt, re-check the receipt.

🛡️ Certifications & Compliance

What you need, who needs it, and where to send it.

🥗

Food Handler Card

All staff — FOH and BOH

Required — Before Day 1

Required for every person who works with food. This is California law.

  • Valid for: 3 years from the issue date
  • How to get it: Complete a Food Handler course online through an ANSI-accredited provider. StateFoodSafety.com or ServSafe.com are both accepted. Takes 1–2 hours, costs ~$15.
  • Where to send: Email a copy to ryan@stadiumpepz.com
🍺

RBS Certificate

FOH staff only (not required for BOH)

Required — Before Bar Work

Required for all FOH staff who serve or handle alcohol. You cannot work the bar without it on file.

  • How to get it: Complete the California RBS training through the ABC-approved portal at abcbiz.abc.ca.gov. Available online, takes a few hours.
  • Where to send: Email your certificate to ryan@stadiumpepz.com
📋

Other Paperwork

Completed with HR on or before your first day

  • I-9 Verification — federal employment eligibility form
  • CA DE-4 / W-4 — payroll withholding forms
  • LWTA Wage Notice — California-required; signed on hire
  • Employee Handbook Acknowledgment — signed receipt of the handbook

Questions? Ask your shift lead or reach out to Ryan.

⭐ Who We Are

Stadium Pepz has been serving Anaheim since 2001. We're a local, family-owned business built on hand-crafted recipes and genuine hospitality. Every guest who walks through our door deserves an upbeat, welcoming experience — from the food to the service to the atmosphere.

We're proud to give back to our community through fundraisers and local initiatives. When you work here, you represent all of that.

🌟 Our Core Values

  • Guest first, always. Our number one priority is providing the best possible customer service, every single shift. A great visit today means a returning guest tomorrow.
  • Fresh quality food. We take pride in hand-crafted recipes made with quality ingredients. That standard starts at the oven and ends at the table.
  • We're a team. When one station is struggling, we all feel it. When we support each other, shifts run smoother and guests notice.
  • Community matters. We're not just a restaurant — we're part of the Anaheim community. How you treat guests reflects on all of us.
  • Own it. If something's wrong — a mistake on an order, an unhappy guest, a station falling behind — speak up. Don't let it fester. Ask for help and fix it fast.
